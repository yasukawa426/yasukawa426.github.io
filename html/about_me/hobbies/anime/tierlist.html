How I Judge Anime

I donâ€™t judge anime just by production quality or how flashy the animation is. Sure, soundtracks, voice actors, and visuals matter, but what really counts for me is enjoyment, vibes, and the characters. I care about how fleshed out the characters are, their dynamics and relationships, and the moments that make me laugh, cry, or think.

For example, Attack on Titan has a well written and complex story, great visuals and soundtracks (the ops are extremely iconic), but I enjoyed Chainsaw Man more . I just love all the characters from the show, their interecations, countless emotional moments. Bro, I'm literally Denji man (jk).

In conclusion, my order of judging criteria would be something like this:

  1. Characters: How fleshed out they are, their relationships, interactions, how I connect with them, etc.
  2. Enjoyment/Fun: Did I laugh, cry, get hyped, think. Emotions in general.
  3. Production Quality: Animation, soundtracks, voice acting. Its not the most important thing for me, but it does add to the experience. Nobody wants to watch a show with Berserk 2016 or Nanatsu no Taizai's season 4 animation quality (even tho I did).
  4. Story/Plot: It doesn't hurt to have a good and complex story. Made in Abyss and Attack on Titan, for example, are great because of it. But as long as the anime doesn't have a horrible story, full of plot holes and inconsistencies, I'm fine with it, if they have something else going for them, of course.

Keep in mind that while they're are in order of importance, ALL of them are important.
